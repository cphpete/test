New Year's Eve

Cocktail Selection

Espresso Martini Bold & Velvety
A decadent cocktail combining the boldness of espresso with the smooth sweetness of coffee liqueur and vodka. Perfect for those who enjoy rich, layered flavors.
Origin: Invented in 1983 by bartender Dick Bradsell at the Soho Brasserie in London, England, originally called the Vodka Espresso.
Ingredients: Vodka, espresso, coffee liqueur, simple syrup.
Spicy Margarita Zesty & Spicy
A bright and bold cocktail that blends the tartness of lime with the warmth of jalapeño heat and a savory Tajin rim. Refreshing and invigorating.
Origin: Inspired by the original Margarita, invented in the late 1930s or early 1940s in Mexico, with a modern spicy twist that became popular in the early 2000s.
Ingredients: Tequila, fresh lime juice, orange liqueur, jalapeño slices, Tajin seasoning.
Hey Mambo Tropical & Bittersweet
A delightful riff on the Piña Colada, combining the tropical sweetness of pineapple and coconut with a bittersweet touch from Aperol. A hint of agave from tequila and mezcal adds depth without overpowering the palate.
Origin: Crafted by Mark Hibbard at Via Vecchia in Portland, Maine, this contemporary creation emerged during the Tiki cocktail resurgence of the late 2010s.
Ingredients: Aperol, mezcal, blanco tequila, pineapple juice, coconut cream, fresh lime juice.
Amaretto Sour Sweet & Citrusy
A smooth and nutty drink with the sweetness of amaretto and the bright, tangy finish of fresh lemon, elevated by a velvety texture.
Origin: Popularized in the 1970s, the modern version with bourbon has become a beloved variation worldwide.
Ingredients: Amaretto, bourbon, fresh lemon juice, simple syrup, egg white.
Whiskey Sour / Rum Sour Rich & Balanced
A timeless cocktail blending the smoothness of whiskey or rum with a touch of citrus and the warm spice of bitters.
Origin: With roots in the 1860s, this cocktail has stood the test of time, offering endless variations.
Ingredients: Whiskey or rum, lemon juice, simple syrup, egg white, Angostura bitters.
